Welcome to the Morphing Wing Project!

If this is your first-ever course at UNSW, a warm welcome we're excited to have you on board. You'll apply your creativity and technical skills using fundamental tools, facilities, and software.

We're looking forward to seeing the creativity, innovation, and teamwork you bring!

Design Specification Reference

Critical dimensions and requirements your design must meet

Wing Dimensions

  • Max Wingspan 300 mm
  • Max Chord Length 150 mm
  • Max Thickness 30 mm
  • Mount Hole Diameter 8 mm
  • Mount Location 25% chord

Electronics

  • Arduino Board Uno R3
  • Servo Type SG90 / MG90S
  • Servo Signal Pin Pin 9
  • Power Supply 5V USB / 6V ext
  • Max Current Draw 500 mA

Wind Tunnel

  • Test Section Size 300 300 mm
  • Wind Speed Range 5 - 25 m/s
  • Test Duration 15 min slot
  • Mount Clearance Min 20 mm
  • Max AoA Range -10 to +15

Installing SOLIDWORKS 2025

In this section, we will talk about the installation of SOLIDWORKS on Windows. Even though UNSW has labs with powerful computers that students can use, some students may wish to install and use the software on their personal computers.

Important: SOLIDWORKS only works on Windows. If you are using a Mac, you will need to set up a Windows environment (e.g., Boot Camp, Parallels, or a virtual machine) or access SOLIDWORKS through the university computer labs.

This license can only be used for your course related activities. Do not share this information.

During installation, ensure you UNTICK "SOLIDWORKS Electrical". Otherwise, you may experience an error related to the "SQL server".

Note: Access to SOLIDWORKS EDU will expire July 31, 2027.

Pre-Testing Checklist

You will not be able to test your wing if it does not meet required specifications.

  • Wind tunnel mount Correct location and installation
  • Morphing wing prototype Fully assembled and functional
  • Electronic and Arduino setup Wired, coded, and tested
  • Servo motor and power supply Connected and operational
  • Team data sheet Ready to record data and wing deflection

CNC & Vacuum Former

Available on Wednesdays only (Weeks 25). After Week 5, access is closed.

Book Now

Steps to Use

  • Book a 30-minute consultation timeslot (Wednesdays only)
  • Prepare your project plan and CAD design before the consultation
  • Present your plan and CAD during the session for review
  • Gain approval from the demonstrator
  • Once approved, proceed with CNC machining or vacuum forming
  • Your demonstrator will handle the CAM setup based on your CAD

Important Deadline

After Week 5, no new groups will be able to access these facilities. Please plan ahead.
